CASTLES IN THE PROVINCES OF PARMA AND PIACENZA

The territory of the provinces of Parma and Piacenza, that the history us delivery enclosed within the ancient borders of the Ducato of the Farnese and the Borbone, can be defined a "layer" of castles.
Culture, art and atmosphere, joined to a great enogastronomical tradition, and constitute the essence of this earth laid between Appennini and the Po, about an hour by car from Milan or half an hour of flight from Rome. Castles and fortresses are to disposition of the visitors long the roads that interlace the two Provinces, on the spurs of mounts, between the necks that degrade towards the plain, in lands close to the great Po river.
Structures historical medieval, renaissance, baroque, judged one of the richest and better conserved in Italy, offer the chance to understand not only the architectonic evolution but the political and social one, of these lands and Italy too.
The Ducato is born in the 1545, with the election as Pope of the cardinal Alexander Farnese with the name of Paul III, that it creates a state for the natural son Pier Luigi.
From then, until the Unit of Italy, for approximately 300 years the representatives of the dynasties of the Farnese and the Borbone succeed themselves to the government of the Ducato, until the Conference of Vienna, when it is delivered to Maria Luigia of Austria, Napoleon's wife. Who will visit the Castles of the Ducato will be able to read the history of the powerful families that have inhabited them, of the great artists who have worked, of the loves and the fights that have rendered them so celebrated.


ITINERARY

1st DAY: ROCCA SANVITALE DI FONTANELLATO
The most ancient part of the rocca, situated to the center of the village, goes back to the 1200.
Encircled from a wide ditch, it has been at first military bastion then, from XVI the century, becomes residence of the Sanvitale family, to which it belonged for five centuries.
Rich in precious frescoes, between which the extraordinary cycle by the Parmigianino (1524) about the myth of Diana and Acteon, it is still today a living house furnished with ancient beautiful pieces and paintings.
The famous 'optical room' represents another surprising idea of the masters to follow, from the inside and by a complicated system of prisms and lenses, the life in the centre of the village.
Locality: Municipality of Fontanellato, 19 km from Parma.

2nd DAY: ROCCA VISCONTEA DI CASTELL’ARQUATO
Propriety of the municipality, the fortress of the Visconti is a great defensive building dating back to the XIVth century.
Situated in Castell'Arquato, an ancient village with the magic of the middle ages, it was built first in 1342 under the governement of the Visconti on the foundations of a very ancient castrum built by the romans in the III century b.C.
Its imposing structure, descending towards the valley, embraces two enclosed spaces, one higher and the other lower.
The higher tower, recently restaured, dominates the whole building and is accessible to the upper floor. In the internal rooms there is a museum about medieval life.
Locality: Municipality of Castell'Arquato, 33 km from Piacenza.

3rd DAY: ROCCA MELI LUPI DI SORAGNA
Built in 1385 by the marquis Bonifacio and Antonio Lupi, feudatories of Soragna, the fortress was transformed during the XVIIth century in a beautyful luxuous house of the Meli Lupi family, who still hold it today.
Mentioned as one of the most exquisite examples of the first barocque, it is still furnished with the original pieces and decorated with frescoes by Nicolò dell¹Abate, Cesare Baglione, Ferdinando and, not less important, Francesco Galli Bibiena.
The building is surrounded by a charming english park with secular woods, statues and a small enchanting lake.
Locality: municipality of Soragna, 28 Km from Parma.

4th DAY: CASTELLO DI TORRECHIARA
Surrounded by two circles of walls the castle, built by Pier Maria Rossi at the end of the XVth century raises on a solitary peak with its two proud towers.
Famous as one of the largest and best preserved castles of the region, it is remarkable for its wonderfully decorated rooms, richly painted by artists like Cesare Baglione and Girolamo Bembo.
Unforgettable the extraordinary cycle of profane paintings in the Golden Room, inspired by the love between Pier Maria and Bianca Pellegrini.
The castle is today propriety of the municipality.
Locality: municipality of Langhirano, 18 km from Parma.
